Decoding Pedagogical Intent in Classroom Discourse via Token-Level Contrastive Learning
Multimodal NLP 
Education Domain 
Hindi / Hinglish 
MintRec Intents 
Proprietary Dataset


Most intent recognition research focuses on transactional domains like customer service or 
smart assistants. EduIntent targets a completely different and underexplored space: 
the educational classroom — where the same utterance carries radically different intent 
depending on tone, pacing, and context. We constructed a proprietary Hindi/Hinglish 
multimodal dataset from real academic discourse scenarios, annotated with fine-grained 
MintRec intents. On top of this, we implemented Token-Level Contrastive Learning with 
Modality-Aware Prompting, a method that aligns acoustic and textual signals at the sub-utterance 
level — something that generic intent classifiers completely ignore. 
The result is a system trained on how learning actually sounds, not just what it means on paper.
